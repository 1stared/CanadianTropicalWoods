Machiche wood, is a durable, strong, and easy-to-work-with hardwood native to the Yucatan Peninsula, Belize, and Guatemala, used in heavy construction, flooring, furniture, and other applications, including railroad crossties.
Machiche is used for furniture components, including legs, frames, and panels.
Its resistance to wear and tear makes it a good choice for flooring. 
Machiche is also used for decking applications. 
High Bending Strength Machiche boasts a high bending strength, even greater than Ipe.
