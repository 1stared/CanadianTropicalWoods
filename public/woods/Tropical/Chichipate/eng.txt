Sweetia panamensis
A tropical timber tree known for its warm brown color, subtle striping, and hardness comparable to American walnut, making it suitable for furniture, flooring, and other woodworking projects. 
It is heartwood is brown to olive with streaks with lighter and darker shades. It has a waxy appearance, medium texture and medium to high luster and takes a high polish. Its lightly figured grain is interlocked and can be roey. The wood has a bitter taste.
Durable, naturally resistant to decay, relatively easy to work with despite its hardness, sands and finishes to a high polish.
